Abstract

In this work, we propose MPS-CLIP, a multi-prompt synergistic CLIP framework tailored for remote sensing image semantic segmentation. By designing a set of complementary visual–textual prompts and integrating them into CLIP’s vision–language alignment mechanism, MPS-CLIP exploits both category-aware priors and spatial structures in high-resolution remote sensing scenes. Specifically, we introduce a multi-level prompt composition strategy to model global scene semantics, local object details, and domain-specific appearance variations. A prompt interaction module is further developed to adaptively fuse information from different prompts, enhancing robustness under label scarcity and cross-domain shifts. Extensive experiments on multiple benchmark datasets demonstrate that MPS-CLIP achieves state-of-the-art performance in both fully-supervised and semi-supervised settings, validating the effectiveness of multi-prompt synergy for remote sensing segmentation.
